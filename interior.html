SERVICES DESIGN PRINCIPALS

The 2018 book, This Is Service Design Doing: Applying Service Design Thinking in the Real World, by Adam Lawrence, Jakob Schneider, Marc Stickdorn, and Markus Edgar Hormess, proposes six service design principles:[26] Human-centred: Consider the experience of all the people affected by the service.[26] Collaborative: Stakeholders of various backgrounds and functions should be actively engaged in the service design process.[26] Iterative: Service design is an exploratory, adaptive, and experimental approach, iterating toward implementation.[26] Sequential: The service should be visualized and orchestrated as a sequence of interrelated actions.[26] Real: Needs should be researched in reality, ideas prototyped in reality, and intangible values evidenced as physical or digital reality.[26] Holistic: Services should sustainably address the needs of all stakeholders through the entire service and across the business.[26] In the 2011 book, This is Service Design Thinking: Basics, Tools, Cases,[27] the first principle is “user-centred”. "User" refers to any user of the service system, including customers and employees. Thus, the authors revised “user-centred” to “human-centred” in their new book, This is service design doing, to clarify that 'human' includes service providers, customers, and all others relevant stakeholders. For instance, service design must consider not only the customer experience, but also the interests of all relevant people in retailing.[27]
